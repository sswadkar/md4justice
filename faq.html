Our entire system is based on the idea that as one united force, we can truly make a difference. Many of the ideas we use for our emails are suggested to us by people. Informing us, using the contact page, about new bills and ideas can help fuel our next proposal.

Inputting your home address or a home address near you allows us to determine who your district’s representative is and it also helps avoid auto-reply emails asking for your address. Representatives often only listen to their constituent's concerns and will ask for your address to confirm you are their constituent. Using a zip code isn’t always accurate, and so, if you were to send an email to a district’s representative that isn’t yours, they might not voice your concerns due to you not being their constituent. This is where the home address comes into play, as using this address will help us return your district’s representative’s email with little to no error.

Once you click send, our website determines your district representative’s email address through a Google service (Google Civic Information). This avoids sending your address anywhere and the process of using your address to find your district’s representative’s email is completely local, which means that it is done on your machine. Your address is also put in the email you are sending so that your representative knows that you are their constituent. If you still wish to not input your address, you can find your district representative’s email address someplace else and input it manually into our site.
